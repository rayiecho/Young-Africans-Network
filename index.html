Who We Are

Empowering African Youth Through Action

Young Africans Network (YAN) is a pan-African, youth-led, volunteer-driven organization founded in Kenya in April 2026. We exist to empower young Africans through education, skills development, mentorship and access to opportunities that enable them to actively build the future together.

We are non-partisan, inclusive and committed to creating lasting impact across Africa and the diaspora.

What We Do

Our Core Programs

Six transformative programs aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals

01
IT Training & Digital Awareness
Equipping youth with digital skills, online safety awareness and 21st century competencies for the digital economy.
SDG 4 — Quality Education
02
Scholarships & Global Opportunities
Increasing access to scholarships, fellowships, internships and global opportunities for African youth worldwide.
SDG 4 & SDG 10
03
Career Development & Public Speaking
Improving employability, confidence and communication skills through CV training, mock interviews and public speaking.
SDG 8 — Decent Work
04
Leadership & 21st Century Skills
Nurturing ethical, future-ready African leaders through workshops, mentorship and leadership development programs.
SDG 16 — Strong Institutions
05
Community Impact & Giving Back
Promoting social responsibility, volunteerism and community service across Africa and the diaspora.
SDG 17 — Partnerships
06
Mental Health & Wellbeing
Promoting youth wellbeing, resilience and emotional awareness through peer support programs and wellness sessions.
SDG 3 — Good Health
