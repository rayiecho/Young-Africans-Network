Journey & Stories

Founding Voices

Words from the Founders

The hearts, minds and souls behind Young Africans Network share their journey, motivation and vision for Africa's future.

Mr. Emmanuel Ikumilu Mwori
Founder & Executive Director
📍 Kenya
Visionary & Builder
"The idea of YAN was not born in a boardroom. It was born in the heart of a young African who refused to accept that our generation was destined to be spectators of our own continent's story."

When I look across Africa, I do not see poverty. I see potential. I do not see problems. I see people — brilliant, resilient, creative young people who lack not capability but opportunity. That is why I founded Young Africans Network.

YAN was born from a simple but powerful conviction: that African youth, when connected, equipped and empowered, can and will transform this continent. We do not need to wait for governments or institutions to give us permission to build. We can start now, with what we have, where we are.

Every young African who joins YAN is not just joining an organization. They are joining a movement — a continental family committed to each other's growth, success and wellbeing. I am honoured to walk this journey with every single one of you.

The future of Africa is not coming. It is here. And you are it.

— Emmanuel Ikumilu Mwori
Highness Lomayani Mollel
Administrator
📍 Tanzania
Mentor & Strategist
"I joined this journey because I believe in the power of young African women and men to rewrite the narrative — not just for themselves, but for generations that will come after us."

Growing up, I often felt that the systems around me were not designed with people like me in mind. Opportunities existed, but they were not equally accessible. Information was available, but not equally distributed. Networks existed, but not everyone was invited in.

YAN is our answer to that reality. It is a deliberate, intentional space where every young African — regardless of country, background or circumstances — is welcomed, valued and equipped to succeed.

What excites me most about YAN is not just what we will achieve, but who we will become in the process. When young Africans come together in pursuit of a shared vision, something magical happens. Friendships are formed, ideas are born, and futures are transformed.

I am proud to be part of this founding team. And I am even more proud of every member who trusts us with their journey.

— Highness Lomayani Mollel
Mr. Innocent Onesmo Nnko
Administrator
📍 Tanzania
Organiser & Enabler
"Africa does not need a generation that waits for change; it needs a generation bold enough to create it through action, collaboration, and purpose."

Africa is home to one of the most powerful and promising generations the world has ever seen. Across the continent are millions of young people filled with creativity, resilience, talent, and vision. The challenge facing African youth is not a lack of ability, but often a lack of opportunity, exposure, and support.

Yet despite these barriers, young Africans continue to rise, innovate, lead, and create solutions that are transforming communities every day. The future of Africa depends on empowering its youth to dream beyond limitations and believe in the value of their ideas and voices.

When young people are educated, connected, and inspired, they become more than individuals pursuing success — they become leaders, changemakers, and builders of stronger societies. There is immense power in young Africans choosing to believe in themselves and in one another.

A continent filled with young people who dare to think big, uplift their communities, and pursue innovation with courage has the ability to rewrite the narrative of Africa for generations to come.

— Innocent Onesmo Nnko
Ms. Debra Chepkorir
Head of Community Impact & Volunteering
📍 Kenya
Community Builder & Servant Leader
"Real change does not start in conference rooms or on stages. It starts in communities — with people who show up, give back, and refuse to leave anyone behind."

I have always believed that the truest measure of a generation is not what it achieves for itself, but what it does for others. Volunteering and community service are not just acts of kindness — they are acts of courage. They say: I see you, I value you, and I am willing to give my time and energy to lift you up.

At YAN, community impact is not a footnote — it is a foundation. We exist not just to empower individual young Africans, but to create a ripple effect that reaches families, communities and entire societies. Every mentor who gives an hour, every volunteer who shows up, every young person who chooses service over comfort — they are all writing a chapter of change.

Africa's transformation will not come from a single leader or a single moment. It will come from thousands of ordinary young people making extraordinary choices every day — choosing to give, to serve, to connect, and to care.

Together, we are not just building an organization. We are building a culture — a culture of Ubuntu, of shared humanity, of a continent that rises together.

— Debra Chepkorir
Mr. Regan Ayiecho
Co-Founder
📍 Kenya
Technologist & Builder
"I do not sleep to dream, I dream to change the World. Technology has the power to connect, to equip and to amplify. YAN exists to ensure that the tools of the future are in the hands of every young African who is ready to use them."

I have always believed that the gap between where African youth are and where they could be is not a gap of talent — it is a gap of access. Access to information. Access to networks. Access to skills. Access to opportunity.

When Emmanuel shared his vision for YAN, I immediately saw it as more than an organization. I saw it as infrastructure — a platform that could systematically close those gaps at scale, across borders, across cultures, across generations.

Building YAN has been one of the most meaningful things I have ever done. Seeing young people from Kenya, Cameroon, Rwanda, South Sudan, Nigeria, Ghana and beyond come together under one roof — united by one purpose — is nothing short of extraordinary.

We are just getting started. The best chapters of this story are yet to be written. And I cannot wait to write them together with all of you.

— Regan Ayiecho

Our Vision

Where We Are Going

"We envision an Africa where every young person has access to the education, skills, networks and opportunities they need to live a life of dignity, purpose and impact — regardless of where they were born."

This vision drives every program we run, every session we host, every opportunity we share and every member we welcome. It is not just a statement — it is a commitment. A promise. A covenant between us and every young African who trusts us with their journey.

Our Journey

The YAN Story So Far

🌱 The Idea is Born
Emmanuel Ikumilu Mwori envisions a pan-African network that connects, equips and empowers young Africans across the continent and diaspora.
Early 2026
🤝 The Founding Team Forms
Emmanuel, Merlene and Regan come together as co-founders, united by a shared vision and a commitment to serve African youth.
March 2026
🏗️ Building the Foundation
8 departments are established, department leads recruited from across Africa, and the organizational structure is set in place.
April 2026
🌍 Members Join from Across Africa
Young Africans from Kenya, Cameroon, Rwanda, Tanzania, South Sudan, Ghana, Nigeria and beyond begin joining the network.
May 2026
🚀 Official Continental Launch
YAN officially launches to the world on 1st June 2026 — marking the beginning of a new era for African youth empowerment.
1 June 2026
✨ The Journey Continues...
Programs, training, scholarships, leadership summits and continental events — the best chapters of the YAN story are still being written.
